Why Liquid-phase electron microscopy of proteins won't work...

Problem 1: Rotational Blurring

Particles in solution move in 3D. Translational motion on the XY plane can be visible in videos, rotational motion is harder to see. I'm going to ignore translational movement because it can be somewhat corrected, and for now only consider rotational blurring.

The natural rotation of particles in solution can be approximated by the following equation: $$\tau_r = \frac{8\pi\eta r^3}{k_BT}$$ Where:

  • KB = Boltzmann constant
  • T = temperature
  • η =Dynamic viscosity of the solvent
  • r = particle radius

This gives the rotational relaxation time - the time after which the orientation is randomised. If the relaxation time is 1s then after 1s, a particle's orientation bares no statistical relationship to where it was a second ago.

Lets look at the effect of radius on relaxation time. For now I am going to assume constant viscosity and temperature.
